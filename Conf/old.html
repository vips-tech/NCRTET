About NCRTET 2025

The NCRTET - 2025 Hybrid Conference (online and offline) is a premier platform for exploring innovations in smart technologies, communication, and robotics. It brings together researchers, professionals, and students to share the latest advancements and network with leading experts. Featuring technical sessions, tutorials, and panels, the conference highlights key areas like high-speed data communication, energy-efficient wireless devices, and GPS-based vehicle positioning. Authors are invited to submit papers, as the event seeks to drive groundbreaking research in telecommunication. Join us to exchange ideas and advance the future of smart technologies.

SCOPUS

If a research paper meets high-quality standards, it becomes eligible for publication in reputable journals and can be recommended for SCOPUS indexing. The process begins with rigorous peer review, where experts in the field evaluate the originality, significance, and technical quality of the research. Once a paper is accepted for publication, it undergoes final editorial checks, formatting, and inclusion in the journal’s database. Journals indexed in SCOPUS ensure wider discoverability, making the research accessible to a global academic audience.

Possible Plagiarism Scenarios
Plagiarism can take various forms, combining multiple factors. Below are key scenarios, starting with the most severe:

1. Uncredited Full or Major Copying (50%+)
Copying a large portion of a paper without citation, quotation marks, or references. Combining sections from multiple sources without attribution.

2. Uncredited Copying Across Multiple Papers (50%+)
Plagiarism spread across several papers by the same author, summing to over 50%.

3. Uncredited Large Copying (20–50%)
Copying a significant portion from another paper without proper citation.

4. Uncredited Copying Across Multiple Papers (20–50%)
Combined plagiarism across papers equating to 20–50% of the shortest paper.

5. Uncredited Copying of Smaller Elements (Up to 20%)
Copying paragraphs, sentences, or illustrations without proper attribution.

6. Uncredited Improper Paraphrasing
Changing only a few words or rearranging sentences without citation.

7. Credited Copying Without Clear Delineation
Providing credit but failing to use quotation marks or formatting to distinguish copied material.
